## CASP Competition and Protein Folding Progress
- The decision was made to enter the CASP competition to assess the quality of ideas and speed up the solution to the protein folding problem, with external competition being a key factor in evaluating progress [(00:51:30)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d95J8yzvjbQ&t=3090s).
- CASP is a community-wide assessment held every 2 years, where teams are given the amino acid sequences of about 100 proteins and try to solve the folding problem using computational methods, with the goal of achieving a score of over 90 to be considered a solution [(00:52:03)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d95J8yzvjbQ&t=3123s).
- The competition involves predicting the structures of proteins that have already been determined by experiments in the laboratory but have not yet been revealed publicly, with the known structures serving as the gold standard for comparison [(00:52:11)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d95J8yzvjbQ&t=3131s).
